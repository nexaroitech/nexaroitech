How Much Does a Business Website Really Cost in India? (2026 Guide)

28 June 2026 · 6 min read · By Nexaroitech Team

"How much will a website cost?" is usually the first question a business owner asks — and one of the hardest to answer with a single number, because the honest answer is: it depends what you actually need.

What drives the price more than anything else

It's rarely the design that drives cost the most — it's the number of pages, the amount of custom functionality (bookings, payments, user accounts), and how much original content needs to be written or organized before development even starts.

Roughly, where different project types tend to land

  • A simple landing page (one page, a form, basic sections) — the fastest and least expensive option, good for a single offer or service.
  • A standard small-business website (5–10 pages: home, about, services, contact, maybe a blog) — the most common request, and the right fit for most local businesses.
  • An e-commerce storefront — costs more due to product catalog setup, payment gateway integration, and inventory considerations.
  • A custom web application (user accounts, dashboards, custom logic) — priced closer to a software project than a "website," since it involves real backend development.

Costs that are easy to forget upfront

  • Domain registration and renewal (small, but recurring)
  • Hosting — varies a lot depending on expected traffic
  • SSL certificates (often included free with modern hosting, but worth confirming)
  • Ongoing maintenance or updates after launch
A website quote that seems unusually low is worth a second look — it often means hosting, revisions, or basic SEO aren't actually included, and get added back later as extra costs.

How to get an accurate quote instead of guessing

The fastest way to get a realistic number is to describe what the site needs to do, not just look like — how many pages, whether you need a contact form, whether visitors need to log in or pay online, and whether you already have your content and images ready.
